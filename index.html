Hello!

I'm a Ph.D. Candidate at the University of South Carolina working in Dr. Josh Stone's Zooplankton Ecology Lab

My research centers on better describing plankton communities. I am interested in the fine-scale spatial and temporal variation which can be extremely important for determining plankton interactions and aquatic food webs. To study plankton, I utilize a variety of classic and novel sampling tools. Much of my work focuses on the application of imaging tools to better measure plankton populations and traits. With these new tools, I am extremely interested in ways new statistical methods can better describe high-frequency plankton measurements.

Outside of my research, I have a lot of fun with R programming language. I'm always excited to chat about code!

Take a look at my current projects to see what I'm working on.
