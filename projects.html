Current Projects

Oligotrophic Plankton Community Structure

The oligotrophic ocean gyres are one of the largest habitats on Earth. While plankton in these areas can be very sparse, their community structure and population dynamics have large impacts ocean foodwebs and biogeochemical cycles. I'm working on several projects investigating topics including Rhizaria, diel vertical migration, and the influence of OMZs.

Community Science Plankton Imaging

Plankton imaging offers a new frontier for improving data quality and frequency for plankton monitoring programs. Collaborating with the planktoscope developers, National Phytoplankton Monitoring groups, and several fantastic undergraduate students, I am working to distribute low-cost imaging tools to community volunteer groups.

Data Management for Plankton Imaging Systems

Plankton imaging systems generate large amounts of data, often scattered across several files in a mess of directories. I'm developing data warehousing processes for our lab to store and analyze the new mass amounts of data we generate. Additionally, I am developing an R package for wrangling and analyzing data which follows the Ecotaxa format.

Estuarine Plankton Responses to Storm Events

Intense rainfall is an increasing stressor to coastal ecosystems in the Southeast US. Inputs of freshwater and anthropogenic run-off can drastically alter phytoplankton communities. However it is unknown how such impacts will affect zooplankton populations. Currently we are developing observational studies to identify the impact of storm events on the whole plankton community.

Past Projects

Phytoplankton Patterns on the Central California Coast

Coastal upwelling systems are eutrophic habitats which support productive, diverse asseblages of phytoplankton. However, phytoplankton abundance and community structure is extremely variable across multiple temporal scales. To fully understand ocean ecosystems, we must understand the drivers of phytoplankton community structure. In this study, my co-authors and I synthesized a dataset consisting of 10-years of phytoplantkon observations. We identified local and regional forces which structure phytoplankton abundance and community composition across seasonal and interannual timescales.
